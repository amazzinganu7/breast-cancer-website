Breast Cancer Information

What is Breast Cancer?

Breast cancer is a disease where cells in the breast grow out of control. It can start in the milk ducts or glands and may spread to other parts of the body if not treated early.

Early Symptoms

  • A lump or thickening in the breast or underarm.
  • Changes in breast size, shape, or skin texture.
  • Nipple discharge or inversion.
  • Redness or swelling.

If you notice any of these, see a doctor right away.

Self-Examination Steps

  1. Stand in front of a mirror and check for changes in shape or skin.
  2. Lift your arms and look for lumps or dimpling.
  3. Lie down and use your fingers to feel for lumps in a circular motion, starting from the outside and moving inward.
  4. Check your underarms and collarbone area.
  5. Do this monthly, especially after your period.

Prevention Practices

  • Maintain a healthy weight and exercise regularly.
  • Limit alcohol and avoid smoking.
  • Get regular mammograms as recommended by your doctor.
  • Know your family history and discuss with a healthcare provider.
